Technical infrastructures exist generally as secondary overlays to pre-existing spatial conditions.

Architecture is at risk of serving simply as a backdrop to immaterial innovation.

Literature Review

Immaterial infrastructures are recognised to influence social organisation, circulation, function, context, public and private space and perception at the urban scale.

Architectural knowledge focused on the influence of immaterial infrastructures on design process and building performance.

Knowledge Gap

Understanding the influence of immaterial infrastructures on the product of architecture and associated spaces.

Research Questions

Main Question

How does a consideration of emerging immaterial infrastructures associated with data, impact on the design of architectural space and its use?

Sub Questions

How is information flow related to shifts in material and spatial thinking in architecture?

How are the interplay of data and physical contexts related to scalar and temporal considerations in architecture?

What influence do non-human and autonomous intelligent systems have on the material outcomes of architecture?

What parallel histories exist regarding the treatment of human inhabitants and the info-sphere in architecture, information technology and media towards new understandings in design?

What is the changing nature of political relationships emerging from the existence of immaterial infrastructures in architecture?
