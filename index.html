Topic Overview

Critical observation thst smart architecture generally exists as a secondary overlay to
pre-existing architectural conditions.

Architecture at risk of operating as just a backdrop for technological and immaterial innovation.

Explore the ways that the material and spatial outcomes of architecture has and can respond.

Immaterial Infrastructures

Defined as the combination of discrete computation, wireless radio wave frequencies, software, and physical networks that facilitate and control data transfer.

Material Outcomes

Refers to the built product of architecture and the physical systems of materials that architects employ to organise and realise coherent spatial conditions.

Research Context

Data and Exchange

Data is the new oil.

Clive Humby and Edwina Dunn, the inventors of the ubiquitous customer loyalty card, famously stated that data was “the new oil”. Their metaphor illustrates how data, as a crude entity, can be gradually refined to produce valuable insight and economic advantage through correlative patterns. This is also known as Big Data.

Architectures Technological Legacy

The Machine Architecture Group - SEEK 1970

Radical speculations on non-human influence via information theory and technology.

Computing considered as either envelope (hardware) or atmosphere (software) towards new social and environmental contingencies (Ripley, Thun, Velikov 2013).

Technological influence beyond construction techniques into the internal workings of buildings and arrangements of material and space (Banham 1984).

Literature Overview

Immaterial infrastructures recognised to influence social organisation, circulation, function, context, public and private space and perception at the urban scale.

Architectural knowledge focused on the influence of immaterial infrastructures on the design process (BIM, data driven decisions) and building performance.

Knowledge Gap

Understanding the influence of immaterial infrastructures on the product of architecture and associated spaces.

A need to consider effects at the building scale.

A mode of operation for architecture to respond to the forces of immaterial infrastructures.

Research Questions

Main Question

How does a consideration of emerging immaterial infrastructures associated with data, impact on the design of architectural space and its use?

Sub Questions

How is information flow related to shifts in material and spatial thinking in architecture?

How are the interplay of data and physical contexts related to scalar and temporal considerations in architecture?

What influence do non-human and autonomous intelligent systems have on the material outcomes of architecture?

What parallel histories exist regarding the treatment of human inhabitants and the info-sphere in architecture, information technology and media towards new understandings in design?

What is the changing nature of political relationships emerging from the existence of immaterial infrastructures in architecture?

Objectives

Interrogate other disciplinary investigations of immaterial infrastructures to apply to architecture.

Understand the types of influence immaterial infrastructures have within and on architecture.

Examine the ways that architecture has and can innovate through spatial means.

Develop an appropriate theoretical framework to test towards a conclusion.
