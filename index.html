Topic Overview

Critical observation in smart architecture that digital infrastructures exist generally as secondary overlays to pre-existing spatial conditions.

Architecture at risk of reducing to a mere backdrop to technological innovation.

Immaterial Infrastructures

Defined as the combination of discrete computation, wireless radio wave frequencies, software, and physical networks that facilitate and control data transfer.

Material Outcomes

Refers to the built product of architecture and the physical systems of materials that architects employ to organise and realise coherent spatial conditions.

Research Context

Data and Exchange

Data is the new oil.

Clive Humby and Edwina Dunn, the inventors of the ubiquitous customer loyalty card, famously stated that data was “the new oil”. Their metaphor illustrates how data, as a crude entity, can be gradually refined to produce valuable insight and economic advantage through correlative patterns. This is also known as Big Data.

Literature Overview

Architectural knowledge focused on the influence of immaterial infrastructures on design process and building performance.

Knowledge Gap

Understanding the influence of immaterial infrastructures on the product of architecture and associated spaces.

A mode of operation for architecture to compete with the effects of immaterial infrastructures.

Research Questions

Main Question

How does a consideration of emerging immaterial infrastructures associated with data, impact on the design of architectural space and its use?

Sub Questions

How is information flow related to shifts in material and spatial thinking in architecture?

How are the interplay of data and physical contexts related to scalar and temporal considerations in architecture?

What influence do non-human and autonomous intelligent systems have on the material outcomes of architecture?

What parallel histories exist regarding the treatment of human inhabitants and the info-sphere in architecture, information technology and media towards new understandings in design?

What is the changing nature of political relationships emerging from the existence of immaterial infrastructures in architecture?
